*A test of Augur's forking mechanism is coming. This will strengthen the oracle, demonstrate the process, and ensure every REP holder is ready for future forks.*

![Augur fork mechanism](/blog/augur-cryptos-first-algorithmic-fork-1.webp)

## ‚öñÔ∏è What is the fork, and why does it matter?

[Micah Zoltu](https://3.go-fund-micah.eth.limo/blog.html), current Augur developer, created a crowdsourcer which raised enough funds to trigger a fork as a planned experiment to test the oracle and dispute system.

([See Micah's post](https://listed.to/authors/33689/posts/66981))

A fork is Augur's ultimate backstop, a codified social defense that makes attacking the system economically irrational.

When oracle disputes reach the threshold (2.5% of REP staked in 1 round), Augur splits into parallel universes for each outcome. Every REP holder must then migrate to the universe they believe reflects reality.

([Learn more](/@lituusfoundation/micahs-augur-fork-0a1494868d97))

That is why **REP is not a passive asset**. Holders must occasionally act to uphold the oracle.

## üìÖ What Happens Next

Micah has closed the crowdsourcer and begun building the decentralized tooling required for the fork.

Once this is ready (soon‚Ñ¢), the process will proceed in two phases:

1. **Dispute Game (~2 months)**
   A market is brought to resolution where REP is staked back and forth on competing outcomes until it is forced into a fork.

2. **Fork (60 days)**
   The universe splits, and all REP holders must migrate to the branch they believe reflects reality. Since this is a test fork, and likely with real forks, the answer will be obvious.
   REP that doesn't migrate during the 60-day window becomes worthless and permanently stranded in the old universe.

A migration UI will be provided for on-chain REP holders to connect their wallets and complete the migration.
